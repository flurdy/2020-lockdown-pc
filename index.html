Why a new PC

  • Macbook Pro was fine before 2020
    • 2014 MBP 13" i7 16GB
  • Pandemic WFH 2020 cooked it
    • Large FP projects
    • Zoom/Slack with Webcam
    • Screen sharing
    • 4K screen(s)
  • B4: Less FP, small microservices
  • B4: Cloud based kubernetes for dev
  • B4: Test in production
  • B4: Not whole stack locally, Pact integration testing not docker compose
  • Now: CPU over 80 degrees
  • Now: Fans went nuts
  • Now: 32" 4k, with another on the way

Specs required

  • Multi-core CPU
  • 32GB+ RAM
  • Fast 1TB+ SSD
  • Graphics card that handles 4K & games
  • MacOS preferred, but could be Linux
  • Not noisy
  • Work and play
  • Preferable 8 or more cores
  • 32GB ok for now, 64GB preferred long term
  • New NVME disks is lightning fast, esp PCIe 4.0 cards
  • Hackintosh

Build process

  • Had two little helpers
  • Slow, short but steady progress each day
  • Time: 11 minutes

Build: Case

Fractal Design Meshify S2
  • Mesh front
  • Very well made
  • Fractal is the volvo of case manufacturers
  • Easy to build in
  • Grommets, cable ties
  • Great air flow
  • Spacious

Build: Case

Fractal Design Meshify S2
  • USB C on front IO

Build: Case

Fractal Design Meshify S2
  • Includes fan hub

Build: Fans

Fractal Design Prisma AL-14 PWM

  • PWM
  • RGB
  • 8 140mm fans
  • 3 intake fans at the front, 1 at the bottom, 1 via PSU
  • 1 exhaust fan at the back and 2 with the top radiator
  • Fan hub was helpful
  • More fans than I need, but can run very quiet
  • Make sure you fit them all the right way...
  • Used two fan power extension cables.

Build: Fans

  • Had to remove part of PSU shroud to fit all 3 fans at the front
  • Bottom black fan was to cover the white bottom chassis

Build: Power supply

BeQuiet Straight Power 11

  • 135mm fan
  • 850 watts
  • Modular
  • Very quiet
  • Plugged in cables later

Build: Motherboard outside

Asus ROG Strix B550-E

  • Slot CPU in and lock it
  • Click in RAM sticks
  • Screw in NVME drive
  • Anti static
  • Make sure CPU aligns
  • Make sure they the right way. Nearly snapped one...
  • NVME heat spreader
  • Manual PDFs on my laptop screen

Build: Fit motherboard

  • IO shield
  • 8 screws
  • Middle screw was not needed

Build: Motherboard

  • Cable management
  • Back of the board
  • Tidy cables

Build: AIO Cooler

Fractal Design Celsius+ 280

  • Attach fans to radiator
  • Time: 14 minutes
  • Comes with same Prisma fans
  • Make sure they are the right way...

Build: AIO Cooler

  • Attach radiator to the top lid
  • Reattach the top lid into the case
  • Helpfully the top lid comes off
  • Very tight fit
  • 280mm is wider than 240 & 360
  • Millimeter clearance from RAM sticks

Build: AIO Cooler

  • Fit AM4 bracket to pump
  • Apply thermal paste
  • Fit pump to CPU
  • Easier said than done
  • Pump comes with preapplied thermal paste
  • I removed the existing thermal paste
  • Applied Arctic MX4 thermal paste
  • Twice
  • The thermal paste wipes was handy

Build: Graphics card

PowerColor Radeon RX 5700 XT 8GB Red Devil

  • Slot in graphics card in top 16x PCIe slot
  • Attach 2 x 8 pin power cables

  • Very popular at the moment
  • Arch, Manjaro

Operating System

Install Pop!_OS 20.04

  • Download ISO from pop.system76.com
  • Make bootable USB key in MacOS with Etcher
  • Insert into new PC and turn on
  • Install onto NVME disk, enable disk encryption
  • Buy super fast 16GB USB 3.1 flash drive
  • Install took 1 minute!
